Gene

Genes are a type of knowledge encoded as DNA inside of living organisms that is subject to evolution.

Replication

Replication of genetic knowledge is not based on how well it helps the organism survive but how much the it helps the gene in its own copying. A lot of knowledge encoded in genes therefore has incredibly limited reach — it always has the same solution to a single problem: it explains how to spread the gene through the population at the expense of its rivals[1].
