Replicator

An abstraction which, once instantiated physically, tends to cause itself to remain so by contributing to its copying[1]. Examples of replicators are: ideas or memes (the golden rule), genes (outrun predators).

Computer programs can also be replicators, as programs that solve a particular problem well get talked about and therefore copied.
